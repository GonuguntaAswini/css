kuchipudi

Kuchipudi is a classical Indian dance-drama from Andhra Pradesh, blending pure dance (Nritta), expressive dance (Nritya), and theatrical acting (Natya). It is known for its quick footwork, fluid movements, subtle expressions, and dramatic storytelling, often incorporating singing and traditional Carnatic music. The performance typically starts with rituals, an invocation, and introductions before moving into technical dance, expressive storytelling, and dramatic action. Performed to Carnatic music, with singing by the dancer also being a distinctive feature.

Bharatanatyam

Bharatanatyam is an ancient, renowned Indian classical dance form from Tamil Nadu, recognized for its intricate footwork, expressive gestures (mudras), and sophisticated storytelling. Rooted in spiritual and divine themes, it draws principles from texts like the Natyashastra and incorporates elements of rhythmic precision (tala), melody (raga), and emotion (bhava). A traditional performance includes specific pieces like Alarippu and Varnam, accompanied by Carnatic music, and is often dedicated to Hindu deities.

kathak

Kathak is one of India's eight major classical dance forms, originating in northern India, particularly Uttar Pradesh, and known for its rhythmic footwork, expressive storytelling, and graceful hand and eye movements. The dance features distinct styles called gharana, with the Jaipur, Lucknow, and Banaras gharanas emphasizing different aspects like intricate footwork, acting, or facial expressions, respectively. Kathak blends Hindu and Muslim cultural influences, incorporating Persian elements, and is performed with instruments such as the tabla and ghungroo bells.

Kathakali

Kathakali is a classical Indian dance-drama form from Kerala, combining dance, music, acting, and mime to tell stories from Hindu epics, characterized by elaborate makeup, vibrant costumes, enormous headdresses, and expressive eye and hand movements (mudras). The form developed in the 17th century and involves male performers who use their facial expressions to convey emotions. The meaning of Kathakali is conveyed through a distinct system of character types (Vesham), each signified by specific colors and makeup.

Manipuri

Manipuri is an ancient classical dance form from India, characterized by graceful, fluid, and circular movements with subtle hand gestures and subdued facial expressions. Its major forms include the devotional Raas Leela (celebrating Krishna and Radha), the vigorous male-dominated Nata-Sankirtan (combining dance with congregational singing), and the martial Thang-Ta (using swords and spears). Other significant forms are Lai Haraoba, the basic folk form from which many stylized dances evolved, and the solo Leima Jagoi performed by women.

Sattriya

Sattriya is an Indian classical dance form from Assam, established in the 15th century by saint Sankaradeva to spread Vaishnavite devotion. It incorporates elements of dance theatre, with two main styles: the masculine Paurashik Bhangi (Tandava) and the feminine Stri Bhangi (Lashya). Key aspects include its devotional repertoire based on Krishna devotion, specific traditional costumes, music from instruments like khols and taals, and a dance vocabulary of hand gestures, footwork, and movements performed by both men and women.
